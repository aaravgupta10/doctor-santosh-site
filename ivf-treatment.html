What is IVF?

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) is a process where an egg is combined with sperm outside the body, in a specialized laboratory. The fertilized egg (embryo) is allowed to grow in a protected environment for 3 to 5 days before being gently transferred into the woman’s uterus to establish a pregnancy.

It is the most effective form of assisted reproductive technology (ART) and is often the solution for blocked fallopian tubes, severe male factor infertility, or unexplained infertility.

The IVF Journey

1

Ovarian Stimulation

Medications are used to encourage the ovaries to produce multiple eggs.

2

Egg Retrieval

A minor, painless procedure to collect the eggs under sedation.

3

Fertilization

Eggs are fertilized with sperm in the lab using IVF or ICSI techniques.

4

Embryo Transfer

The best quality embryo is placed into the uterus to begin pregnancy.
