Fertility Treatments

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI)

A less invasive, first-line fertility treatment optimizing the timing and placement of sperm to increase conception chances.

What is IUI?

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI), often called artificial insemination, is a fertility treatment that involves placing sperm inside a woman's uterus to facilitate fertilization. The goal is to increase the number of sperm that reach the fallopian tubes and subsequently increase the chance of fertilization.

Dr. Santosh Gupta often recommends IUI as a starting point for:

  • Mild Male Factor Infertility
  • Unexplained Infertility
  • Cervical Factor Infertility
  • Couples using Donor Sperm

The Process

How IUI Works

1

Monitoring

We monitor your cycle closely using ultrasound to predict ovulation. Mild medication may be used to stimulate egg growth.

2

Sperm Washing

The sperm sample is "washed" in our lab to separate high-quality, motile sperm from other seminal fluids.

3

Insemination

The washed sperm is inserted directly into the uterus using a thin catheter. It is a quick, nearly painless procedure.

Benefits of IUI

Less Invasive

IUI involves no surgery and causes minimal discomfort, similar to a pap smear.

Cost-Effective

IUI is significantly more affordable than IVF and is often the first step in fertility treatment.

Quick Procedure

The insemination takes only a few minutes, and you can resume normal activities almost immediately.
